Directions
With the Reset assessments we address two types of obstacles to change: 1) the client has a problem but either doesn’t recognize it or is in denial about it; and, 2) the client recognizes there is a problem but due to certain beliefs or attitudes isn’t doing anything about it. To gain more information about these two types of obstacles, the core assessments first ask the client to self-report on whether they believe they are experiencing a problem. The root cause assessments then ask the client to self-report on various beliefs or attitudes that may be interfering with correcting the problem. In the report above the assessments are color-coded as follows
Green the assessment was not flagged, indicating the client does not see a problem
Yellow the assessment was borderline for flagging, indicating the client feels there may be a problem or is ambivalent about whether there is a problem
Red the assessment was flagged, indicating the client recognizes there is a problem
